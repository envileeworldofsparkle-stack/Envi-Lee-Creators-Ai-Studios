Brand Pitch Email
What makes a winning pitch
Lead with THEM, not you
Open by referencing something specific about the brand. Shows you're not copy-pasting 100 emails.
Numbers build trust
Include specific audience size, engagement rate, and a past result — not just "I have a big following."
Keep it under 200 words
Brand managers get 50+ pitches a day. Short and scannable wins. Save the detail for the follow-up.
End with a clear next step
"Would you be open to a 15-min call?" or "I'd love to send over my media kit." — always drive an action.

DM Pitch
DM strategy
3 sentences max
Nobody reads a long DM from a stranger. Say who you are, why you're reaching out, and what you want them to do next.
Don't pitch the deal in the DM
The goal of a DM is to get them to ask for your media kit or email. Close the deal in the email, not the DM.
Engage before you DM
Like their last 3 posts. Comment something genuine on their latest. Then DM 24h later — they'll recognize you.
Follow up once
If no reply in 5 days, send one follow-up: "Hey [brand], just bumping this up — would love to connect!" Nothing more.

How to handle common scenarios
"We'd love to gift you products"
Gifting only = no. Ask what their paid rate is. "I love your brand — do you have a budget for paid content alongside the gifting?"
"What are your rates?"
Don't just send numbers. Ask about their campaign goals first — then tailor your package to what they need. Higher perceived value = higher rate.
"Your rate is too high"
Never drop your rate immediately. Ask what their budget is. Then offer a smaller deliverable at your rate — not a discount on the same work.
"Can we do affiliate only?"
Affiliate-only = no guaranteed payment. Decline or counter: "I do affiliate on top of a flat fee — I don't create content for free in exchange for potential commission."

Contract Review
Common contract red flags
Perpetual usage rights
They own your content forever for any purpose. Push for 1-2 year limit and specific platform restrictions.
No kill fee clause
If they can cancel anytime with no penalty, you could do 80% of the work and get nothing. Always require a kill fee.
Unlimited revisions
If the contract says "revisions until brand is satisfied" — that's unlimited free labor. Cap it at 2 rounds max.
Exclusivity without extra pay
Exclusivity means you can't work with competitors. That costs you money — charge 25-50% extra for it.
Payment "on performance"
Never accept deals where full payment depends on views or results. Charge a flat fee — you can't control algorithms.

How to approach brand outreach
Volume is a system, not a spray
Target 10 brands/week. Research each one before pitching. Personalization beats volume every time.
Find the right contact
Search "[Brand] influencer marketing manager" on LinkedIn. Don't email the general contact form — it disappears.
Track your follow-ups
Follow up once after 5 days. Most deals close on the 2nd or 3rd contact, not the first.
Create FOMO
Mention other brands you work with. "I'm currently in conversations with 2 fashion brands this quarter." Scarcity closes deals.
